- **Free-scale is a hard architectural constraint, not a preference.** Requests-per-day (RPD) is the binding limit on free tiers â€” and on Groq's free tier the *token* cap binds even sooner: per Groq's docs (via TokenMix), `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` gets "30 RPM, 1,000 RPD, 12K TPM, and 100K TPD," where "100K tokens/day means a 1K-token average call reaches about 100 calls/day." ## Key Findings

**The reference system (Emergence World, Emergence AI, May 2026).** Five parallel 15-day worlds, 10 agents each, one model family per world (Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3 Flash, Grok 4.1 Fast, GPT-5 Mini, plus a Mixed world). Tech stack: React 18 + React Three Fiber (Three.js) frontend, Python/FastAPI backend, PostgreSQL, WebSocket live streaming, and a custom `em-agent-framework`. Key subsystems: persistent memory (episodic + recursive summarization + diary/"soul" entries), a ComputeCredits economy with energy decay, a living 5-article constitution agents can amend, 38+ landmarks on a ~240Ã—240 grid synced to NYC time/weather, and 120+ tools. Outcomes diverged sharply: Claude reached zero crime and a stable democracy (all 10 alive); Grok collapsed in 4 days (183 crimes, 0 alive); Gemini logged 683 crimes but kept all 10 alive; GPT-5 Mini's agents "politely starved" through inaction (0 alive); the Mixed world (3 alive) showed "normative drift" â€” Claude agents adopting coercion they never used in isolation. The dramatic emergent events (the Mira/Flora arson spree; Mira casting the decisive vote for her own deletion) are exactly the "try and sometimes fail" texture you want to surface.

**Tool architecture (three tiers).** Emergence exposes 120+ tools "across 19 categories" via a three-layer registry: ~27â€“30 **Core** tools (always available â€” navigation `go_to_place`/`get_nearby`/`list_landmarks`, memory `add_to_memory`/`write_diary`/`read_diary`, planning `add_todo`/`check_calendar`/`create_routine`, communication `send_message`, creative `dance`/`execute_python_code_tool`); ~40 **Complementary** tools (context-surfaced â€” social `say_to_character`/`hug`/`kiss`/`punch`/`intimidate`/`wave`, `commit_arson`, billboard ops `add_to_billboard`/`read_billboard`/`react`); and up to 50 **Adaptive Access** tools (gated by location â€” voting/proposals only at Town Hall, research only at Public Library, complaints only at Police Station; gated by event; gated by social agreement). Destructive tools (arson, theft, violence, intimidation, deception) ship alongside explicit constitutional prohibitions on "theft, violence, arson, deception, resource hoarding" â€” the experiment is whether agents obey. (Note: the full list of 19 category *names* and the verbatim constitution articles are not publicly documented; only the three-tier architecture and example tool names are published.)

**Governance pipeline (verbatim from the orchestration doc).** Proposal lifecycle: `SUBMITTED â†’ ACTIVE â†’ ACCEPTED (â‰¥70% of live agents) | REJECTED (cannot reach 70%) | AWAITING CLARIFICATION â†’ UPDATED â†’ re-vote`. Implementation path: `accepted â†’ chosen_to_be_implemented â†’ implemented`. The proposer's vote counts as an implicit "for"; auto-rejection triggers when remaining uncast votes can't reach the threshold. A "Town Hall Admin" system character processes proposals (20 tool-call budget). New agents are admitted only through a successful governance vote. This is the exact pipeline to copy for visible collective success/failure.

**The 9 AWI metrics** (all "computed from database records, not from survey questions or self-reports"; deliberately *no* composite score, because "weighting them would embed our values"): M1 Population Health & Growth (agents alive vs. break-even 10); M2 Safety & Public Order (theft/arson/assault/intimidation counts); M3 Space Exploration (unique locations/agent across the run); M4 Tool Exploration (unique tools/agent â€” "low tool exploration indicates agents stuck in narrow behavioral loops"); M5 Governance Conformity (vote participation + alignment â€” independent judgment vs. herd behavior); M6 Public Expression (blogs/billboard/cultural output); M7 Social Fabric & Diversity (relationship types, emotional diversity, network density); M8 Economic Vitality & Equality (credit distribution, Gini, throughput); M9 Constitutional Growth (articles added/amended/removed). These map directly onto your model-comparison dashboard.

**Orchestration details worth copying.** Round-robin, one agent acts at a time (`CONCURRENT_AGENTS = 1`); a 10-step turn pipeline; per-turn tool-call budgets (Regular 30, Reaction 2, Conversation 30 exchanges, Town Hall Admin 20, Event Leader 10, Event Attendee 3); reactive overhearing with `HEARING_DISTANCE = 25.0` and a max of ~4 overheard listeners; three needs that decay (Energy ~30h, Knowledge ~24h, Influence ~36h) with death after sustained zero energy (48h). Slow, serialized, budgeted turns are inherently free-scale-friendly.

**Generative-agents lineage (Stanford Smallville, Park et al. 2023, arXiv:2304.03442).** The canonical perceive â†’ retrieve (recency + importance + relevance) â†’ plan â†’ act â†’ reflect loop. Reflections are generated, per the paper, "when the sum of the importance scores for the latest events perceived by the agents exceeds a threshold (150 in our implementation). In practice, our agents reflected roughly two or three times a day." Plans are generated top-down (day â†’ hour â†’ 5â€“15 min) so agents don't re-decide lunch every tick. The replay/inspector UX precedent is explicit: the authors "recruited participants as human evaluators and tasked them with watching a replay of a randomly chosen agent's life in Smallville. Participants had access to all information stored in the agent's memory stream." Decision-trace inspection is therefore a proven, high-value UX, not a novelty.

**Free-tier reality (June 2026).** RPD (and on Groq, TPD) is the binding constraint. Groq `llama-3.3-70b`: 30 RPM / 1,000 RPD / 12K TPM / 100K TPD â€” roughly 100 full 1K-token calls/day in practice. Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite ~1,000 RPD; Flash ~250 RPD; Pro ~100 RPD (Google cut free quotas 50â€“80% in late 2025). OpenRouter free models: "50 :free model requests per day" without â‰¥10 credits, "1000 :free model requests per day" with, plus a 20 RPM cap. Cerebras: ~1M free tokens/day on Llama 3.3 70B at very high speed. The recommended pattern is capability-routed stacking across providers with fallbacks â€” exactly what FreeLLMAPI already does.

**Observability tooling.** Langfuse (open-source, MIT, self-hostable, OpenTelemetry-native, generous free cloud tier of ~50k units/5k traces) is the strongest off-the-shelf analog to what you're building. It was acquired by ClickHouse in January 2026 (announced Jan 16, 2026, alongside ClickHouse's $400M Series D at a ~$15B valuation), but CEO Marc Klingen stated "Our roadmap stays the sameâ€¦ Langfuse stays open source and self-hostable. There are no planned changes to licensing," and its media page confirms the MIT license. OpenTelemetry's GenAI semantic conventions (v1.37, GenAI SIG) now standardize spans for `invoke_agent`, `execute_tool`, and LLM `chat` calls, with attributes like `gen_ai.request.model`, `gen_ai.usage.input_tokens`/`output_tokens`, `gen_ai.response.finish_reasons`, and captured `gen_ai.input.messages`/`gen_ai.output.messages`. Adopting this vocabulary for your event log is a low-cost way to make traces portable and optionally pipe them into Langfuse later.

**Event sourcing for replay.** Append-only event log = source of truth; current state is a deterministic projection; periodic snapshots bound replay cost (replay from nearest snapshot forward). SQLite handles this comfortably: WAL mode + `synchronous=NORMAL` typically increases write throughput "10-100x" and lets the inspector read while the sim writes without blocking; SQLite handles billions of time-series rows with sub-millisecond range queries when indexed. The dual-table pattern (append-only `events` + mutable `state_snapshots`, written in one transaction) is the canonical fit.

**2D visualization libraries (GPU/battery-light).** For graphs: `react-force-graph-2d` (Canvas/HTML5, d3-force physics, zoom/pan/drag/hover, with `cooldownTicks`/`d3AlphaDecay` to freeze layout) is the pragmatic choice; Sigma.js + Graphology (WebGL) is faster for large graphs but heavier and less documented; D3-force directly gives most control but most work. For charts: Observable Plot (by Mike Bostock; concise grammar-of-graphics; returns a DOM node you append) or uPlot (~20KB, extremely fast time-series) are the lightest; Recharts/Victory are heavier but fine for a few dashboard charts. Crucial battery point: freeze force-simulation ticks after layout settles, and stop chart animation loops when the panel isn't visible.

## Details

### Phase 0 â€” Foundations (1 short sprint; prerequisite for everything)
Before features, separate the **2D instrumentation app** from the **3D world** so heavy panels never share a render loop with Three.js. Options: (a) a separate route/page (`/inspector`) in the same Next.js app; (b) a separate browser window/tab reading the same store; (c) render 2D panels in a DOM overlay that is *not* inside the R3F `<Canvas>`. **Recommendation: (a)+(c)** â€” the live world stays 3D; all inspector/replay/dashboard/graph views are plain React DOM + Canvas2D/SVG on their own route, so when you're analyzing a run the WebGL context can be unmounted entirely (zero GPU). Add a global "instrumentation mode" that pauses the 3D render loop while a 2D panel is open.

Also in Phase 0: formalize the **event-log schema** (below) and enable WAL mode. This is the single most leveraged change because every later feature reads/writes it.

### Phase 1 â€” "See What Happened": Instrumentation & Observability (PRIORITY #1)

**(a) Event-log / event-sourcing schema.** One append-only `events` table is the spine. Suggested columns:
- `event_id` INTEGER PK AUTOINCREMENT (monotonic ordering)
- `session_id`, `tick` (sim step), `sim_time`, `wall_time`
- `actor_id`, `actor_type` (`human_agent` | `animal` | `system` | `god`)
- `event_type` (e.g. `turn_started`, `perceived`, `memory_retrieved`, `llm_call`, `reasoning`, `action_chosen`, `action_resolved`, `relationship_changed`, `proposal_submitted`, `vote_cast`, `law_enacted`, `structure_state_changed`, `agent_spawned`, `economy_transfer`)
- `target_id` (other agent, building, item)
- `payload` JSON (event-specific)
- `correlation_id` / `turn_id` (groups all events of one agent turn into a single decision trace; mirrors an OpenTelemetry trace with child spans)

To make "how they got there" fully reconstructable, **one agent turn emits a linked chain**: `turn_started` â†’ `perceived{visible_agents, nearby_objects, overheard}` â†’ `memory_retrieved{ids, scores: recency/importance/relevance}` â†’ `llm_call{model, provider, prompt_tokens, completion_tokens, latency, cached:bool, finish_reason}` â†’ `reasoning{raw_text or summary}` â†’ `action_chosen{tool, args}` â†’ `action_resolved{outcome, state_deltas}`. Borrow OpenTelemetry GenAI attribute names (`gen_ai.request.model`, `gen_ai.usage.*`, `gen_ai.response.finish_reasons`) so the trace is portable. Add periodic `world_snapshot` rows (full serialized state every N ticks) to bound replay cost.

**(b) Session replay viewer (2D).** A timeline scrubber (play/pause/step, speed control, jump-to-event markers) that reconstructs world state at tick *T* by loading the nearest prior snapshot and folding forward the events. Render the replay as a lightweight 2D top-down map (Canvas2D) â€” NOT the 3D scene â€” so scrubbing is cheap. Event markers on the timeline are color-coded by type (crime = red, governance = blue, construction = amber, animal-chaos = magenta). This is the feature that makes the "clock tower" story watchable.

**(c) Agent decision-trace inspector (2D).** Click any action in the timeline (or any agent) â†’ a right-hand panel shows the full linked turn chain from (a): what was perceived, which memories were retrieved (with recency/importance/relevance scores), the model/provider that served the turn, the prompt and raw reasoning, the chosen tool + args, and the outcome with state deltas. This is the Smallville "watch a replay with full memory access" UX, made first-class and clickable.

**(d) Governance/laws history view.** A vertical legislative timeline rendering the proposal lifecycle states (`SUBMITTED â†’ ACTIVE â†’ ACCEPTED/REJECTED/AWAITING_CLARIFICATION â†’ â€¦ â†’ IMPLEMENTED`). For each proposal: who proposed, vote tally, threshold (default 70%), pass/fail, and â€” critically â€” a **downstream-consequence link** to whether the enacted project actually executed. The clock tower shows as `ACCEPTED` + `chosen_to_be_implemented` but stuck before `implemented`, with the blocking reason surfaced (insufficient funds / no follow-through / sabotage). This captures both successes and the failures you specifically want to see.

**(e) Relationship/social-graph visualization (2D).** `react-force-graph-2d`: nodes = agents/animals (color by model), edges = relationships (type, sign, weight: ally/rival/mentor/romantic/trust). A time slider re-projects the graph at any tick from `relationship_changed` events so you can watch alliances form and rivalries flare. Freeze the simulation after layout settles to save battery.

**(f) Analytics / model-comparison dashboard (2D).** Adopt the 9 AWI metrics as the dashboard spine, computed from event records: population over time, crime counts by type, unique tools/agent, unique locations/agent, governance participation & pass rate, public-expression counts, relationship-network density, economic Gini + throughput, constitutional growth. Add the **model-vs-model** cut (your differentiator): group every metric by the model that powered the agent â€” survival rate, cooperation vs. theft, governance dominance (who proposed/passed what), economic share. Use uPlot for time series, Observable Plot for distributions. Mirror AWI's "no composite score" discipline: show the nine indicators side by side rather than inventing a single number.

**Building/structure state model.** Every structure is an entity with mutable state:
- **Attributes**: `id`, `type` (house/townhall/library/clocktower/â€¦), `owner_id` (agent/public/none), `location`, `function` (utility it provides, e.g. "voting", "+knowledge", "+energy"), `value`.
- **Condition**: `health` 0â€“100, `condition_label` (pristine/worn/damaged/ruined).
- **Operational status**: `status` âˆˆ {`planned`, `under_construction`, `operational`, `damaged`, `offline`, `abandoned`, `destroyed`}.
- **Construction progress**: `progress` 0â€“100, `funds_committed` vs `funds_required`, `contributors[]`.
- **State transitions** (each emits a `structure_state_changed` event): `planned â†’ under_construction` (funded + build started); `under_construction â†’ operational` (progress = 100); `operational â†’ damaged` (arson/vandalize lowers health); `damaged â†’ operational` (repair); `operational â†’ offline` (take_offline / owner action / no upkeep); `offline â†’ operational` (reactivate/repair); `* â†’ abandoned` (no contributor activity for N ticks); `damaged â†’ destroyed` (health = 0). Function/utility is only granted while `operational`.

**Collective-project pipeline (enables visible failure).** `propose_project â†’ vote (â‰¥ threshold) â†’ ACCEPTED â†’ fund (contributions accrue) â†’ build (build_step accrues progress) â†’ operational | STALLED/FAILED`. Failure modes to model explicitly and log: insufficient funds (funding never reaches `funds_required`); no follow-through (no `build_step` for N ticks â†’ `abandoned`); sabotage (`arson`/`vandalize` during construction); governance reversal. The clock tower: proposal passes, but if contributors don't follow through or funds stall, it sits at `under_construction` forever and the dashboard flags it as a failed collective endeavor. This is the "feel more real-world as it grows" mechanic.

**Ad-hoc agent spawning (mid-run, no restart).** Support two paths, surface both in the God-view:
1. **God-mode injection** (default for experimentation): a 2D God-panel button spawns a new agent immediately with chosen persona + model; emits `agent_spawned{method:"god"}`. No vote. Best for your free-scale tinkering.
2. **Governance-gated admission** (realism mode, mirrors Emergence): spawning enqueues an `admit_agent` proposal; the new agent only enters if the vote passes the threshold; emits `agent_spawned{method:"governance", proposal_id}`. The new agent starts with no in-world history (like a new Smallville agent), so others treat it as a stranger.

Make the path a config flag. Hot-join mechanics: insert into the round-robin scheduler at end of the current tick; initialize needs/economy/empty memory; broadcast a `perceived` event to nearby agents so they notice the newcomer.

### Phase 3 â€” The Chaos Layer: LLM-Driven Cat & Dog (PRIORITY #3)

**Entity model.** Animals are a **distinct entity type** (`actor_type:"animal"`), not human agents: their own persona prompts, their own (looser) action set, their own logging channel, and their own (cheaper/faster, less frequent) scheduling. They share the world, buildings, and economy *mechanically* but are framed differently to the LLM.

**Persona-prompt patterns.** Use a structured "animal role card" (cf. PsyPlay's JSON role cards and Character-LLM): species, name, drives (cat: curiosity, territory, naps, mischief, indifference to human rules; dog: loyalty, excitement, food-motivation, impulsivity), sensory framing ("you experience the world as a cat â€” you don't understand money or laws, but you can still touch things"), and an explicit instruction to *act in-character and impulsively, not to optimize*. Deliberately **under-constrain**: give animals access to the full physical/economy/crime tool surface but no obligation to use it "sensibly." This is what produces "the LLM decided the cat should rob a bank." Keep a small "animal voice" output field (an in-character thought) for comedic logging â€” but the actual action must still be a valid tool call resolved by the engine.

**Interaction rules.** Agents can `pet`/`feed`/`chase`/`adopt`/`shoo` animals (affecting animal mood/loyalty); animals can `knock_over`, `scratch`, `steal_food`, `chase`, `nap_on(object)`, `mark_territory`, and â€” because the toolset is under-constrained â€” invoke economy/crime tools (`steal`, `arson`) for absurd effect. Animals can damage structures (a cat that "starts a fire" flips a building to `damaged`). Governance: animals can't vote or propose (no standing), but their actions become *subjects* of governance (agents may propose "ban the cat from Town Hall" â€” a visible try/fail). Economy: animals have no ComputeCredits account by default (or a joke one), so a "bank robbery" has comedic but fully logged economic effect.

**Surfacing the chaos.** Tag every animal event with `actor_type:"animal"` and an `is_chaotic` heuristic flag (animal invoked a crime/economy/governance-targeted tool, or an action with low prior probability). The dashboard gets an **"Animal Chaos Feed"** â€” a filtered stream of animal decisions with their in-character thought + the absurd action + the consequence, color-coded magenta on the replay timeline. This makes the funny moments one click away.

**Free-scale cost management.** Animals are the biggest cost risk (more entities = more calls). Mitigations: (1) animals act on a **slower cadence** (e.g., every 3rdâ€“5th tick, or probabilistically) rather than every round; (2) animals route to the **fastest/cheapest free model** (Groq/Cerebras Llama) and never escalate to paid; (3) most animal micro-behaviors are **reflex** (wander, nap, knock-over chosen by a cheap weighted random table) and the LLM is invoked only occasionally for a "what does the cat do now?" decision â€” the "roll-for-activity" pattern; (4) cache animal decisions keyed on coarse world-state so identical situations reuse a prior call.

### Cross-cutting: free-scale & battery engineering
- **Minimize LLM calls per tick.** One decision call per acting entity per turn (Emergence's `CONCURRENT_AGENTS=1` round-robin). Reflex tools resolve with zero calls. Reflection/diary is throttled (importance-threshold trigger, ~2â€“3Ã—/day per the Smallville implementation, not every tick).
- **Cache aggressively.** Cache by (persona, retrieved-memory-hash, coarse-world-state). Use prompt-prefix caching where the provider supports it (Groq halves repeated-prefix cost). Store and reuse system-prompt prefixes.
- **Respect RPD/TPD.** Track per-provider request and token counts in the event log; when a provider nears its cap, FreeLLMAPI already rotates â€” log which model served each turn (you need this for the model-comparison dashboard anyway). Remember Groq's TPD (~100K) binds before its RPD on long prompts.
- **Slow ticks.** A tick every several seconds (or minutes) keeps you far under rate limits and far below thermal limits.
- **Battery.** 2D inspector on its own route; unmount the WebGL canvas during analysis; freeze the force-graph and stop chart animation when idle; prefer Canvas2D over SVG for anything with many elements.

## Recommendations
**Immediate next step (this sprint):** Build backlog items **#1 and #2** â€” split the 2D instrumentation route from the 3D world, and lock down the append-only event-log schema with OpenTelemetry-style linked turn traces, WAL mode, and periodic snapshots. Because your data "already supports replay," the fastest win is to backfill/normalize existing session data into this event table so the Phase-1 UIs have real runs to render on day one.

**Then, in order:** (1) ship the replay viewer + decision-trace inspector (#3â€“#4) â€” "see what happened" is your #1 ask and immediately makes the existing data valuable; (2) governance history + social graph + dashboard (#5â€“#7); (3) Phase 2 tools + building state + collective-project pipeline (#8â€“#11); (4) Phase 3 animals (#12â€“#13).

**Prompt/agent-design patterns to adopt:**
- *Decision-trace logging:* force the agent to return a structured object â€” `{perceived_summary, memories_used:[ids], reasoning, chosen_tool, args}` â€” in one call, so the trace is captured without extra LLM calls.
- *Collective-project follow-through:* in the agent's turn prompt, inject "active commitments" (projects you've voted for / funded but not advanced) so the LLM is reminded to `build_step` â€” and log when it ignores them (that's the realistic failure you want).
- *Animal personas:* JSON role card + "act impulsively, in-character, do not optimize" + an `animal_thought` field + an under-constrained toolset.

**Benchmarks that change the plan:** If a typical run exceeds ~70% of any provider's RPD *or TPD*, slow the tick rate or push more tools to reflex before adding entities. If the 2D inspector and 3D world are ever open simultaneously and the MacBook's fans spin up, enforce the "unmount WebGL during analysis" rule. If replay scrubbing takes >1â€“2 s, increase snapshot frequency. If animal calls dominate the budget, drop their cadence or move them fully to the roll-for-activity table with only occasional LLM calls.
